CIViC variants are usually genomic alterations, including single nucleotide variants (SNVs), insertion/deletion events (indels), copy number alterations (CNV's), structural variants (SVs), and other events that differ from the "normal" genome. In some cases a CIViC variantmay represent events of the transcriptome or proteome. For example, 'expression' or 'over-expression' is a valid variant. Regardless of the variant, it must have an asserted predictive, prognostic or diagnostic clinical relevance to be entered in CIViC. New curators should generally avoid proposing variant names that are unlike any already in CIViC for a different gene.
