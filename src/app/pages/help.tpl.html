Understanding the CIViC data model

At the heart of CIViC is the clinical evidence statement. The clinical evidence statement is a piece of information that has been manually curated from trustable medical literature about a variant or genomic 'event' that has implications in cancer diagnosis, prognosis, or predictive response to therapy. For example, “Patients with BRAF V600 mutations respond well to the drug dabrafenib”. A variant may be a single nucleotide substitution, a small insertion or deletion, a chromosomal rearrangment, an RNA gene fusion, an RNA expression pattern (e.g. over-expression), and so on. Each clinical evidence statement corresponds to a single citable publication.

The following tables define the minimal components and definitions required for a CIViC evidence statement. Once enough clinical evidence statements have been curated, a variant summary is generated describing the variant's significance in cancer, along with current treatment methods. A gene level summary then synthesizes the variant summaries. Each variant can belong to one or more groups. For example 'FGFR fusions' is a group that contains several gene fusions where FGFR2 (or FGFR3) is involved in a fusion with various 3' partner genes.

Breaking down the evidence statement

Interaction type Evidence direction Clinical significance Example
Predictive - Evidence pertaining to a variant’s effect on therapeutic response Supports: The experiment or study supports this variant’s response to a drug Positive: Variant is associated with positive response to treatment (e.g. sensitivity to drug) Breast cancer cell lines with a PIK3CA H1047R mutation showed increased sensitivity to CH5132799 compared to cells with wild-type PIK3CA gene
Negative: Variant is associated with negative treatment response (e.g. resistance to drug) MAP2K1 P124S confers increased resistance to inhibition by AZD6244 inhibition by about 5-fold
Does not support: The experiment or study does not support, or was inconclusive of an interaction between the variant and a drug Positive: Variant is associated with positive response to treatment (e.g. sensitivity to drug) BRAF V600E mutation does not predict response in patients treated with dacarbazine or temozolomide
N/A: Variant does not inform clinical action This drug is not shown to have an impact (sensitivity or resistance) on cell lines with mutation X
Negative: Variant is associated with negative treatment response (e.g. resistance to drug) In a cohort of 100 patients, mutation X is not shown to confer resistance to drug Y
Diagnostic - Evidence pertaining to a variant’s impact on patient diagnosis Supports: The experiment or study supports variant’s impact on the diagnosis of disease or subtype Positive: Variant is associated with diagnosis of disease or subtype BRAF V600E is shown to be associated with the tall-cell variant of papillary thyroid carcinoma (PTC)
Negative: Variant is associated with the lack of diagnosis of disease or subtype Patients with mutation X are found not to have disease subtype A (i.e. mutation X rules out a diagnosis of subtype A)
Does not support: The experiment or study does not support the variant’s impact on diagnosis of disease or subtype Positive: Variant is associated with diagnosis of disease or subtype Study 1 found that, contrary to what was previously believed, mutation X does in fact show a correlation with disease subtype A
Negative: Variant is associated with the lack of diagnosis of disease or subtype Study 1 found that, contrary to what was previously believed, mutation X does NOT in fact show a correlation with disease subtype A
Prognostic - Evidence pertaining to a variant’s impact on disease progression, severity, or patient survival Supports: The experiment or study supports a variant’s impact on prognostic outcome Good Outcome: Variant is associated with a better overall patient outcome In AML, patients with IDH2 R140K mutation have improved overall survival compared to those with wild-type IDH2
Poor Outcome: Variant is associated with a worse overall patient outcome Meta-analysis of studies involving cytogenetically normal, younger patients showed increased overall and relapse-free survival for patients with NPM1 mutations
Does not support: The experiment or study does not support a prognostic association between variant and outcome Good Outcome: Variant is associated with a better overall patient outcome Mutation X was not shown to be associated with improved overall survival
N/A: Variant does not inform clinical action Size of the FLT3-ITD mutant duplication had no impact on overall survival or relapse rate
Poor Outcome: Variant is associated with a worse overall patient outcome Mutation X was not shown to be associated with decreased overall survival

Evidence levels: overview

Level of Evidence Definition Example
A - validated association Proven/consensus association in human medicine A meta-analysis of clinical studies showed that harboring a BRAF V600E mutation predicts worse prognosis in patients with colorectal cancer
B - clinical evidence Clinical trial or other primary patient data supports association In non-small-cell lung cancer patients with EGFR T790M and other activating mutations, their progression-free survival is shorter than those who do not have T790M mutations
C - preclinical evidence In vivo or in vitro models support association Experiments showed that AG1296 is effective in triggering apoptosis in cells with the FLT3 internal tandem repeat
D - inferential association Indirect evidence Glioma cells harboring IDH1 mutation may be more susceptible to chemotherapy or radiotherapy due to their reduced ability to respond to oxidative stress
